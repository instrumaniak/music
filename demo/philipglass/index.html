About

Philip Glass is an American composer. He is considered one of the most influential music composer of the late 20th century. His music is often controversially described as minimal music. Apart from writing music, Glass also regularly performs his own music on keyboards with his ensemble. He has written operas, musical theatre works, ten symphonies, eleven concertos, solo works, chamber music including seven string quartets and instrumental sonatas, and film scores. Three of his film scores have been nominated for Academy Awards.

Timeline

  • 1937: Glass was born on January 31, 1937, in Baltimore, Maryland.
  • 1964: Glass received a Fulbright Scholarship and went to Paris, where he studied with the eminent composition teacher Nadia Boulanger.
  • 1975: A seminal work- 'Einstein on the Beach' was Composed.
  • 1982: He wrote his most famous film score to date for Koyaanisqatsi.
  • 1988: Glass composed one of his most influencial music- a set of five movements titled Metamorphosis.
  • 1990: Made a collaborative recording project with Ravi Shankar which resulted in the album Passages (1990).
  • 2002: Glass wrote film scores that reached out to and influenced a wider global audience. (Martin Scorsese's Kundun, 1997, Godfrey Reggio's Naqoyqatsi, 2002, and Stephen Daldry's The Hours, 2002
  • 2007: A Major work 'Songs and Poems for Solo Cello' was composed for the cellist Wendy Sutter.
  • 2012: He finished his Symphony No. 10.
  • 2015: Glass published his memoir 'Words Without Music'.
